Primary Reading List

Field: Digital Humanities

This primary list establishes the theoretical landscape for analyzing generative AI and the Digital Humanities methodology planned to be used in the dissertation. It is organized to move from the foundational concepts of making and authorship to the broader societal and philosophical implications of AI, including labor, agency, and literacy

I. Critical Making & Design Methodologies

This group establishes "critical making" as a core methodology of my dissertation. Ratto's text defines the field, while Piaget and Papert's work on constructionism provides the pedagogical lineage. The other texts offer contemporary applications, linking the hands-on, critical ethos of making to the specific challenges and affordances of AI. They ground a research-through-building method for my AI‑assisted game prototype.

II. Authorship, Copyright & Labor

These texts trace the concept of authorship from its legal and historical origins to its current state of flux in the digital age. Rose and Boyle provide the essential background on the invention of copyright and the public domain. Potts extends this inquiry to the internet age, setting the stage for a critical discussion of how generative AI further complicates traditional notions of creation, ownership, and intellectual property.

Boyle, J. The Public Domain: Enclosing the Commons of the Mind. Yale UP, 2008.
